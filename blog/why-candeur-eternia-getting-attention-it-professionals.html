Why Candeur Eternia is Getting Attention
from Hyderabad's IT Professionals

A look at why this Bachupally project ticks every box that Hyderabad's tech professionals care about when buying a home.

Project Review • June 3, 2026 • ~8 min read

Walk through any premium gated community in Bachupally right now and you'll notice something: a high proportion of prospective buyers and new residents are IT professionals — software engineers, product managers, tech leads, and startup founders from the Hitech City and Gachibowli ecosystem. Candeur Eternia, in particular, has attracted significant interest from this demographic. Here's a detailed look at why.

What IT Professionals Look for When Buying a Home

Hyderabad's IT workforce has a distinct set of priorities when evaluating a home purchase. They tend to be analytical, research-driven buyers who evaluate on multiple criteria simultaneously rather than making purely emotional decisions:

  • Manageable commute: For most IT professionals, a commute beyond 40 minutes each way is a dealbreaker. The ideal is under 25 minutes with a reliable, predictable route — not one that varies dramatically by traffic.
  • Premium amenities: Having come from Bengaluru or moved from Kondapur/Gachibowli rentals, IT buyers know what good amenity infrastructure looks like and won't settle for a single-room "gym" and an undersized pool.
  • Quality finishes: Vitrified tile flooring, branded fittings, modular kitchen provisions, and proper waterproofing are expected. They've seen enough poorly finished apartments to know what to look for.
  • RERA compliance: IT professionals do their due diligence. A non-RERA project simply won't make it past first-pass evaluation for this buyer profile.
  • Investment upside: With stock market exposure and financial literacy higher than average, IT buyers think carefully about capital appreciation and resale potential.
  • Cash flow-friendly payment plan: Monthly salaries mean a construction-linked plan that aligns with regular income cycles is strongly preferred over lump-sum demands.

How Candeur Eternia Ticks Every Box

The Commute: 5 Minutes to ORR = 20 Minutes to Hitech City

This is the single most important factor for IT buyers, and Candeur Eternia's location in Bachupally delivers it with precision. The project is approximately 5 minutes from ORR Exit 4 (Mallampet Interchange). Once on the ORR, it's a smooth 20-minute drive to Hitech City and 30 minutes to Gachibowli's Financial District — without traffic lights, junctions, or unpredictable congestion.

Compare this to apartments in Kondapur at ₹9,000–₹10,500 per sq.ft that are "10 minutes closer" to Hitech City but cost 40–60% more. For most IT professionals doing the math, a 10-minute longer commute in exchange for 40% savings in property price is an obvious trade — especially when those savings compound as investment returns over 7–10 years.

Additionally, the Miyapur Metro station is 12 minutes away, giving residents a Metro backup for rainy-season commutes or days when they prefer not to drive.

The Amenities: 68,000 Sq.Ft of Genuine Resort Living

IT professionals who have lived in Bengaluru's premium gated communities — Prestige, Brigade, Sobha — bring high expectations to Hyderabad. Candeur Eternia's 68,000+ sq.ft amenity footprint on 7 acres is genuinely in that league:

  • Two grand clubhouses with a combined area exceeding the amenity blocks of most competitor projects in Bachupally
  • Multiple swimming pools including a lap pool and a leisure pool
  • A preview theatre — the "home cinema for the whole community" that doubles as a community hall for screenings and events
  • A full-service restaurant and café within the complex — rare in Hyderabad's gated communities
  • Rooftop lounge with panoramic views — popular with the professional demographic for weekend evenings
  • Fully equipped gym with professional-grade equipment
  • Indoor badminton courts, squash court, and table tennis
  • Landscaped jogging track on the complex perimeter
  • Children's play area and dedicated senior citizen zone

For an IT professional coming home from a demanding workday, having a restaurant, gym, and entertainment venue within the compound — without getting back into a car — is a significant quality-of-life upgrade.

Premium Finishes That Match Expectations

Candeur Eternia's specification sheet reads like a premium Bengaluru project: vitrified tiles in living and dining areas, anti-skid tiles in bathrooms, concealed wiring with modular switches, granite kitchen counter provision, UPVC windows, and high-speed elevators. The master bedroom includes a walk-in wardrobe area — a feature IT couples specifically look for, especially when both partners are working professionals.

RERA Verified — Non-Negotiable for This Buyer Profile

Candeur Eternia is fully RERA registered. For an IT professional who spends 20 minutes researching any consumer purchase, this is simply table stakes — but it's worth stating clearly because a surprising number of Bachupally projects still operate without RERA registration.

The 20+30+50 Plan Aligns with IT Salary Cycles

This is subtler than it looks. IT professionals in India are typically paid monthly and often have annual variable components (bonuses, ESOPs, performance pay) that create predictable liquidity events. The 20+30+50 plan aligns beautifully with this:

  • 20% at booking can come from savings accumulated over 2–3 years
  • 30% during construction aligns with ongoing monthly salary savings over 18–24 months — no single large outflow
  • 50% at possession is typically structured as a home loan — EMI replaces rent, meaning the net cash outflow change is manageable

A buyer purchasing a ₹1.8 Crore 3 BHK pays ₹36 Lakhs at booking, ₹54 Lakhs over construction (₹2.25 Lakhs/month average), and funds the remaining ₹90 Lakhs via a home loan with an EMI of approximately ₹78,000/month — well within the range of a senior IT professional earning ₹2–₹3 Lakhs per month.

Floor Plan Analysis: Which Configuration Works Best for IT Buyers?

IT Couples (No Children Yet)

The 2.5 BHK (1,500–1,700 sq.ft) is the most popular configuration for IT professional couples in their late 20s to early 30s. The extra half-bedroom serves as a dedicated home office — a must in the hybrid work era — while the primary bedroom and living area are generously sized. Monthly EMI on a 2.5 BHK is typically ₹55,000–₹65,000, feasible on a dual-income household.

IT Families (1–2 Children)

The 3 BHK (1,681–2,192 sq.ft) is the logical upgrade when children arrive or are planned. The third bedroom serves as either a children's room or a permanent home office, and the larger living space accommodates family life comfortably. At Candeur Eternia, the 1,978 sq.ft 3 BHK with servant quarter is particularly popular with families who have live-in help.

Senior IT Professionals and NRIs

For NRIs or senior professionals investing from abroad, the premium 3 BHK (2,192 sq.ft) offers the best combination of size, rental income potential, and long-term appreciation. Monthly rentals of ₹38,000–₹48,000 for a furnished 3 BHK in Bachupally represent a gross yield of 2.5–3% — respectable for a city that has seen 15–20% capital appreciation annually.

The Investment Case for IT Professionals

For an IT professional thinking about Candeur Eternia not just as a home but as an investment, the numbers are compelling. Bachupally is appreciating at 15–20% per year in premium projects. A ₹1.8 Crore apartment bought today could be worth ₹2.6–₹2.9 Crore in 4 years — a ₹80 Lakh to ₹1.1 Crore gain on a roughly ₹36 Lakh equity investment (the booking amount). That's a return profile that compares favourably with most equity investments, with the added benefit of living in the asset.

For those who plan to rent it out before moving in, expect ₹35,000–₹45,000 per month in rent for a furnished 3 BHK in Bachupally by possession date — covering the home loan EMI substantially.
