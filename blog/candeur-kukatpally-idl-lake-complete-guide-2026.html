EOI & OTP: What These Booking Options Mean for Buyers

Kukatpally IDL Lake offers two distinct booking mechanisms — EOI (Expression of Interest) and OTP (One-Time Payment). Understanding the difference helps you choose the right approach based on your timeline and budget.

What is EOI (Expression of Interest)?

An EOI is a nominal refundable amount you pay to register your interest in the project and lock in a specific price point. It does not obligate you to purchase, but it gives you priority access when formal bookings open. Think of it as reserving your place in line at today's pricing, even if the formal launch happens weeks or months later.

  • Pay a small token amount (typically ₹2–5 lakhs) to register
  • Get priority in tower, floor, and unit selection
  • Lock the current price — protected from any price revisions before launch
  • Fully refundable if you decide not to proceed
  • First access to lake-view and premium corner units

What is OTP (One-Time Payment)?

OTP is a payment structure where the buyer pays the entire amount (or a substantial portion) upfront at the time of booking. This typically unlocks additional discounts and the best possible pricing because the developer receives funds early in the construction cycle.

  • Pay a significant amount upfront to lock the lowest possible price
  • Get the best per-square-foot rate — typically 5–8% lower than construction-linked plans
  • Ideal for buyers who have funds ready and want maximum value
  • Best suited for investors looking at maximum capital appreciation
